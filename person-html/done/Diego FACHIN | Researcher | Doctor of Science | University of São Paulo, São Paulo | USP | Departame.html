Introduction
PhD (2018) in Entomology at the University of São Paulo, Brazil. I am currently a postdoc researcher, funded by CAPES, at the Faculdade de Filosofia, Ciências e Letras de Ribeirão Preto, University of São Paulo, developing studies on evolution, systematics, taxonomy, and biogeography of lower brachyceran flies (Insecta: Diptera), with special focus on families Austroleptidae, Pantophthalmidae, Stratiomyidae, Xylomyidae, and Xylophagidae.

Diversity and systematics of Stratiomyidae (Diptera) in the Amazon: focusing on the Hermetiinae and curating the collection of the Museu Paraense Emílio Goeldi
Project
This project aims to contribute to the knowledge of Stratiomyidae in the Amazon region, particularly with a study of (i) the fauna of Hermetiinae focusing on the genera Hermetia in the Neotropical region; and (ii) the phylogenetic relationships among species of the genus. Because there is no taxonomic revision of Hermetia, as well as studies for the relationships between its species, this study will contribute to a better understanding of the evolution within the group. This project also intends to curate the collection of Stratiomyidae of the Museu Paraense Emílio Goeldi.

Cladistic analysis of the subfamily Sarginae (Diptera: Stratiomyidae)
Project
The family Stratiomyidae has more than 2,800 described species in 377 genera in the world, placed in twelve subfamilies. The subfamily Sarginae is one of the most diverse groups, with more than 560 described species in 21 genera. Although Sarginae has been indicated as a monophyletic group, almost nothing has been proposed on the phylogenetic relationships among its members. The absence of a global treatment of diversity in the subfamily resulted the maintenance of some paraphyletic genera, as has been indicated for Merosargus related to Acrochaeta, and also is the case for Sargus as pointed out for many authors. Moreover, most of its entire fauna is poorly studied (especially its Neotropical and Afrotropical fauna) so it is needed to evaluate the hypothesis of monophyly of its genera and providing good characters to recognize them. Thus, the main goal of this project is to reconstruct the phylogenetic relationships within the subfamily Sarginae using morphological data.

Total curation of the insect collections of the Museo Nacional de Historia Natural del Paraguay
Project
The long term goal of this project is to have a fully curated and species-level identified entomological collection which could bring quality information about the species inhabiting Paraguay, accompannied by a specialized library that could enable ongoing and future research on specific groups by the starter researchers and other colleagues from Paraguay and abroad. A fully curated and identified collection will hopefully encourage students to engage in insect research in a country where systematic entomology is an almost unexistent workfield. The current work consists in gathering the necessary taxonomic literature enabling the correct identification of target taxa, mostly entire families, thus building an important reference library. A second step consists of identifying the material already housed in the museum (mostly from generalcollectings made during the decades of 1980 and 1990), trying to reach identifications as finer as possible (ideally to species level). A third step is the publication of ilustrated catalogs for the museum's material of each already curated family, as a way of sharing mportant information for any future use, and as a way to encourage students to join the project. Some more elaborated products will include descriptions of new taxa, full regional catalogs, identification keys and illustrated identification guides.
