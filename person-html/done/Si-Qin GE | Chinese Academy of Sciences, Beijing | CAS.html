Multiple Loci Barcoding Pollinator Insects in China
Project
1. Sample pollinator insects and pollinated plants to gather both tissue and images; 2. Observe and collect other biological informations including natural history of pollinator insects; 3. Sequencing multiple loci of both pollinator insects; 4. In-depth morphological study to gather functional traits of pollinator insects; 5. In-depth taxonomic study to be sure of morphology-based species identities; 6. Developing a novel protocol to integrate and analyze multiple loci DNA barcoding and functional traits. Target loci include standard COI, 28S D2 and CytB as well as those comics data from Next Generation Sequencing. The leading PI in the Institute of Zoology has been Dr. Douglas Chesters. He's been focusing on a few big and species rich genus of Apoidea to develop the procol. We welcome more collaborations from morphology-based taxonomists on Lasioglossum, Hylaeus and Megachile. We are happy to help and host visitors/postdocs who has been active on bee studies or bioinformatics on molecular taxonomy. We are also keen to invite morphology taxonomists to work together with us.
