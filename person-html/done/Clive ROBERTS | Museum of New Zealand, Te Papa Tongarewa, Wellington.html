Project (1)
Functional biodiversity of New Zealand's marine fishes versus depth and latitude
Project
Biologists know least about the largest habitat on earth - the deep sea. The contrast from energetic shallow waters to the relatively large, stable, homogeneous environment of the deep ocean offers an exceptional opportunity to investigate potential mechanisms driving biodiversity. We will measure functional traits important for locomotion and food acquisition of 144 species of New Zealand fishes vs depth, using both museum specimens and unique in situ stereo-video footage. Our footage was drawn from a stratified replicated ecological study design, spanning 21 degrees of latitude from subtropical to subantarctic waters and depths from 50m to 1200m, allowing rigorous spatial inferences to be made for the first time. We have characterised patterns in key traits across depth and latitude (Myers et al., in press), and are currently working on quantifying patterns of functional alpha and beta diversity across depth and latitude.
