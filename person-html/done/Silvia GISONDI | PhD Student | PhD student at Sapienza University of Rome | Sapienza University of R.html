Introduction
I am a PhD student working at Sapienza University of Rome and Copenhagen University. I am interested in Diptera systematics and phylogeny, mainly concerning Calyptratae and the oestroid clade more specifically. My PhD project deals with the phylogeny of Rhinophoridae and Polleniidae using both morphological and molecular evidences.

Phylogeny of Rhinophoridae and Polleniinae (Diptera): toward the evolution of the Oestroidea
Project
Rhinophoridae and Polleniidae are two families of Calyptrate flies including all parasitoid species: while rhinophorids are the only insects able to exploit crustaceans (Isopoda: Onyscidea) as hosts, Polleniinae feed on earthworms. Rhinophorids lack of adult autopomorphies supporting monophyly, but their larval stage is amazingly interesting and hightly derived. Polleniins has always been considered a subfamily within the Calliphoridae, a widely known polyphiletic/paraphyletic group but lately they have been recognized at a full family status. The phylogeny of both these families is tricky since they have never been investigated in a proper way: this is the main goal of my project. During next three years I aim at obtaining suitable molecular data to join with morphological ones in order to get a robust phylogeny of these two groups of flies. This PhD project is co-funded by the University of Copenhagen (Denmark).
