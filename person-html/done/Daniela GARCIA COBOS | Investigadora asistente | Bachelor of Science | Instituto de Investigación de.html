Project (1)
Herpetological Collections of the Biological Resources Research Institute Alexander von Humboldt
Project
The Herpetological Collections of the Alexander von Humboldt Biological Resources Research Institute (IAvH-Am) was founded in 1968 from specimens obtained during the formation of the National System of Protected Areas by the now-defunct National Institute of Renewable Resources and Environment (INDERENA), which allowed its consolidation until 1994. This reference collection is one of the largest and most representative of Colombia. Its growth has been associated with several national and international projects, including inventories made by the now-defunct Humboldt Institute Exploration and Monitoring Group (GEMA) through 2009. The information provided by our Amphibian collection includes scientific knowledge Reflects on more than 80 products between scientific articles and books. In addition, this reference collection has generated contributions for the development of several undergraduate and graduate studies and investigations of national and foreign students and researchers. Since the middle of 2014, the collection has been under evaluation, restoration, and growth; in order to improve and guarantee the quality of the geographic and taxonomic information that it currently contains. Until November 2020 the collection has 16,692 specimens in the Amphibian collection (Currently IAvH-Am = formerly IND-Am), while the collection of reptiles has 9,301 specimens (Currently IAvH-R= formerly IND-R). The objective of the Institute's amphibian collection is to participate implicitly and explicitly in the generation of biological information in three guidelines, which involve: 1) the discovery and description of taxa to consolidate the national inventory of amphibian diversity in Colombia, 2) generate data and quality information to understand the evolution and risk factors of loss and decline of this group, 3) manage the knowledge linked to reference collections to communicate it to science and society and generate criteria for Decision-making.
